## Powerups and Equipment

Last time, we covered skills and techniques, which are the building blocks for customizing characters and creatures in the Pipes and Plumbers system. As promised, today we'll finally take a look at items, which can be divided into two categories: consumable items, which we'll call “powerups”, and worn items, which we'll call “equipment”.

### Powerups

Let's take a look at powerups first. A powerup has two key characteristics. First, it must be actively used, or activated - eat a mushroom, drink a tonic, toss a rock candy. Second, it is consumed when used, or after a specified number of uses (unless otherwise noted, all powerups are consumed after a single use).

Here's a sample powerup:

> ##### Froggie Drink
>
> A drink with the logo of the Juice Bar in Tadpole Pond, so you know it's good.
>
> **Effect:** All allies regain one lost hit point.

Any creature can use a powerup it is carrying as an action. Some powerups, such as the Froggie Drink above, affect all allies or all foes. Most, however, only affect the user.

Alternatively, a creature can use a powerup on an adjacent creature as an action (or a creature within range, if so noted in the powerup description). For example, a creature can use a Mushroom to heal an adjacent ally, or throw a Yoshi Cookie to take down a foe.

Some creatures may drop powerups when they are defeated, or powerups may be found in chests in the world. In either case, powerups may be picked up freely when not in combat, or by spending an action during combat. Characters can also toss a powerup they are carrying to an ally as an action.

### Equipment

Like powerups, equipment falls into the general category of “items”, but there are several key differences. One is preparation: rather than gaining the effect immediately on use, equipment must be used or “equipped” ahead of time; conversely, as long as the item is equipped, the user gains the benefit of that equipment. That brings us to the other key difference - equipment isn't consumed by use, but remains in effect until un-equipped (and can be carried and re-equipped at a later point if so desired).

Each item of equipment belongs to one of three categories: weapons, armor, and accessories. We'll take a look at each of these categories below.

#### Weapons

A weapon typically grants the user an attack-related skill, such as a hammer swing or a shell kick. It may also provide miscellaneous bonuses. Here's a sample weapon:

> ##### Punch Gloves
>
> *Weapon.* A pair of padded gloves.
>
> **Effect:** As an attack action, you can make two punch attacks against an adjacent enemy. Each attack inflicts one point of damage on a successful Attack check and an additional +1 point of damage on a critical hit.

You'll notice that the weapon doesn't just grant a flat bonus to attack roll or damage. Pipes and Plumbers is a very simple system, but that doesn't mean there can't be some interesting variety.

#### Armor

Armor typically grants the wearer a defensive bonus, sometimes at the cost of reducing the wearer's speed. Armor can also grant miscellaneous bonuses. Here's a sample armor item:

> ##### Shy Mask
>
> *Armor.* A simple mask with a leather strap.
>
> **Effect:** You gain a +1 bonus to Defense and Magic Defense.

This armor is relatively simple, but more advanced armor might grant bonuses to certain attacks (a Hammer Bros. suit), to magic spells (a Magikoopa robe), or even the ability to float or turn invisible (a Boo Guy mask).

##### Accessories

Accessories provide a miscellaneous benefit of some kind. Here are a couple of examples:

> ##### Jump Shoes
>
> *Accessory.* A pair of worn shoes that a plumber might wear.
>
> **Effect:** You can use the Jump skill. If you already have the Jump skill, you inflict 1 additional point of damage on a successful check.

> ##### Feather
>
> *Accessory.* A colorful feather. You feel lighter just holding this item.
>
> **Effect:** While this item is equipped, you gain +1 Speed.

Accessories can apply bonuses, grant techniques, and otherwise help characters beyond the effects of weapons and armor.

### Carrying and Using Equipment

Rules for equipping items are simple: each character can equip one weapon, one armor item, and one accessory. Equipping or removing an item during combat requires an action, and can be done freely outside of combat; switching from an equipped item to an item in inventory (see below) is considered equipping an item, and costs only a single action.

Characters can carry up to three extra pieces of equipment, for a total of six items between equipped items and inventory. In order to acquire additional items, gear currently being held or used must be discarded.
