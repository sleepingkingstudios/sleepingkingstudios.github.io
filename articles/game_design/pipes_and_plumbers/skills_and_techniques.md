## Skills and Techniques

Last time, we covered Hit Points and Flower Points, which measure a creature's vitality and vigor during combat. In a nutshell, hit points (or “hp”) represent the creature's ability to absorb damage, are reduced when the creature takes damage, and when a creature's hp reaches 0 they are knocked out. Flower points (or “fp”) represent the creature's ability to dish out special abilities, are reduced when the creature uses those abilities, and when a creature's fp reaches 0, they cannot use those abilities.

### Skills

With that covered, we can finally turn our attention to skills. In the Pipes and Plumbers mini-system, creatures are defined by two things - their attribute values, which are a general measure of aptitude, and their skills.

There are actually two types of skill. The first are referred to as just skills, and typically grant a specific or conditional bonus. Here is a sample Skill:

> ##### Turtle On A Half-Shell
>
> You wear a Koopa Shell as if it were made to fit you (since, you know, it was).
>
> **Requirements:** None.
>
> **Effect:** You can wear a Koopa Shell as armor.
>
> **Notes:** Koopa automatically gain this skill as a bonus skill.

Each skill has a *title* (in our case, “Turtle In A Half-Shell”), a *description* (the text right below the title), and an *effect*. Some skills have *requirements*, which are prerequisites that must be met before a creature can learn or use the skill; if the skill has no requirements, that field can be omitted. Finally, the *notes* section provides some additional context or information about the skill; if there are no notes, the field can be omitted.

### Techniques

Techniques are a special type of skill - rather than providing a bonus, a technique allows the creature to take a new action in combat. Here's a sample Technique:

> ##### Drain
>
> *Technique.* You can drain the life from an enemy with a successful special attack.
>
> **Cost:** 1 Flower Point.
>
> **Effect:** On a successful Special Attack check, inflict 1 point of damage on target creature. If damage is inflicted, you heal 1 point of damage.

Techniques have an additional field, *cost*. In order to use the technique, the creature must spend the specified number of flower points. You can see that the requirements and notes fields are omitted; this skill does not have any specific requirements. Also, note the “technique” at the start of the description: this is a *keyword*, which indicates that special rules apply to this skill.

### Gaining Skills

Since Pipes and Plumbers is just a mini-system, an incunabula if you will, we're not going to go into much detail on character or creature creation, or gaining abilities during play. For the moment, let's say that each player must choose a species for their character (e.g. a Goomba, a Koopa, a Shy Guy, etc), which will grant them a couple of skills. We'll go into a bit more detail on species later.

Players may also choose one bonus skill, and may be granted an additional bonus skill at key points in the game (such as defeating a mini-boss). The main avenue for gaining and swapping skills, though, will be through equipment: in Pipes and Plumbers, equipment grants the wearer skills and/or techniques.
