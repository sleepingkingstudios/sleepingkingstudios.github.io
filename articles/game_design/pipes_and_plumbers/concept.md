## Concept

The core plot is based on the principle of narrative inversion, or taking a familiar element and considering its complement or opposite. In our case, our plot is a straightforward reversal of the core Mario story. In short, the Princess has kidnapped Bowser! And it's up to the Koopa Troop to defeat the Princess, rescue Bowser, and save Mushroom Kingdom. Why? There's an evil star or something. Those details can be worked out later.

This solves some key issues with setting an RPG in the Mushroom Kingdom: first, there will be no arguing over who gets to be Mario! Second, a game where the players control the traditional heroes would not offer much ability to customise their characters. After all, Mario's abilities are fairly well known - he can jump, he can shoot fireballs, sometimes he uses powerups like a Hammer or Kuriba's Shoe. Conversely, if the characters play a Goomba, a Koopa Troopa, a Magikoopa, and a Shy Guy, there's room for considerable variety.

### The Mechanics

The concept also fits well with the plan of generating a minimal system to build upon. In the Mario universe, abilities tend to come from items and powerups rather than complicated classes and ability progressions. We will be adapting this to our RPG - there are no character levels, or classes, or (at least initially) experience or character progression.

In fact, a character's mechanics will be defined by only two things - attributes and skills. Both of these will be fleshed out in future posts, but in a nutshell a character's attributes are its numerical values - hit points, flower points (a localisation of mana points), and a handful of stat values used for making checks - attack, defense, magic attack, and so on.

Skills, on the other hand, are either a conditional bonus (e.g. gain an extra +1 to Defense while wearing a Koopa shell) or a specific thing the character can do (jump on an enemy, shoot a fireball, and so on). The plan is to allow each player to choose a couple of skill cards for basic abilities, and then augment those with skills gained from equipment (weapons, armor, etc). Over the course of a session, the characters will get the opportunity to find improved gear, and may earn additional skill cards from overcoming challenges (e.g. a boss fight or a key story milestone).

So that's the basics for the character model. Next time, we'll take a more in-depth look at the combat mechanics.
