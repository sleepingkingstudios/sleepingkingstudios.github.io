## Combat Basics

In previous installments, we've covered the concept for the Pipes and Plumbers mini-system, introduced our task resolution heuristic, aka "the Check", and started to flesh out our character and creature mechanics with the introduction of Attributes. Today, we'll start in on the basic combat mechanics.

### Rounds and Actions

The combat mechanic in Pipes and Plumbers is built upon Rounds and Actions. In brief, an action is a discrete thing a creature or character can do - attack an enemy, cast a spell, move around the battlefield. When running a combat encounter, each participant takes one action during their turn; once all participants have acted, play continues back to the first participant again and continues until the combat has concluded. A round is one cycle of actions, starting with the first participant's action and ending with the last's.

#### Getting Started

To start a combat, each participant rolls a Speed check (a check to which the player adds his or her character's speed attribute value). The participants will act in the order of their check results, starting with the highest result. In case of a tie, the creature with the highest speed value goes first; if both have the same speed value, flip a coin.

#### Combat Actions

Each round, a creature gets to take one action from the following list:

- **Attack An Enemy:** To attack an enemy, the creature must succeed at an Attack check, with a difficulty equal to 3 + the target's Defense value. On a successful attack, the enemy takes damage and may suffer other effects, based on the weapon used (we'll cover weapons and equipment at a later point).

- **Use A Skill:** A Skill is a specific thing a creature can do - jump on an enemy, throw a fireball, and so on. The requirements, the check needed, and the effect on a success are all determined by the specific skill chosen.

- **Use A Power-Up:** The creature can use any power-up in their inventory and gain the associated benefits. The effect is determined by the power-up chosen.

- **Move:** The creature can move to another point on the battlefield. Where and how far the creature can move depend on the creature's Speed, any movement-related skills and/or bonuses (such as a Koopa Troopa shell that grants wings), and the discretion of the Narrator.

- **Take A Miscellaneous Action:** A miscellaneous action is anything not covered above, such as listening for enemies, bashing down a door, or opening a treasure chest. Whether a miscellaneous action can be done as part of another Action, whether it requires a full Action to perform, or whether it can be done at all in the space of a round are left to the Narrator's discretion.
